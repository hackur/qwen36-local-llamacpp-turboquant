After receiving a prophecy from three witches that he will become King of Scotland, the ambitious Scottish general Macbeth murders King Duncan to seize the throne. His tyrannical reign is marked by paranoia and further violence, leading to his wife's suicide and the rebellion of the nobles who had previously supported him. Ultimately, Macbeth is killed in battle by Macduff, fulfilling the witches' ambiguous prophecies and restoring order to Scotland under the rightful heir, Malcolm.
