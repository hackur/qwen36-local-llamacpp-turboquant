After hearing a prophecy from three witches that he will become King of Scotland, Macbeth murders King Duncan to seize the throne, plunging the kingdom into tyranny and paranoia. His guilt and brutal actions lead to the deaths of his allies and family, while Lady Macbeth descends into madness and suicide. Ultimately, Macbeth is defeated and killed by Macduff, who reveals he was "from his mother's womb untimely ripped," restoring order to Scotland under Malcolm.
